# PVA App Improvement Prompt

Copy and paste this prompt into a new conversation when you want feature improvement suggestions:

---

## The Prompt

You are a product design consultant specialising in gamified self-discipline and habit-tracking apps. I need you to analyse my app and suggest improvements based on what the highest-rated apps in this space do well.

### My App: PVA (Person, Vehicle, Assignment)

**Concept:** A gamified daily discipline app built around the PVA framework — Person (spirit, soul, body), Vehicle (family, community, church), and Assignment (work, PhD, ministry). It's rooted in Christian faith and designed for holistic life stewardship.

**Current Features:**
- Daily task checklist across 4 pillars (Person, People, Process, Product)
- XP system with levelling (Seed → Sapling → Sprout → Tree → Oak → Redwood → Sequoia → Ancient → Eternal → Ascended)
- Skeleton avatar that evolves with level progression
- Daily State score (resets each day, purely reflects today's completion %)
- Temple XP that grows with consistency but decays when days are missed (below 50% completion)
- Streak tracking with streak freeze option
- HP (Health Points) system
- Evening Reflection modal with 1-5 rating and journal
- Chat-style routine builder (onboarding) with tappable answer options
- My Routine tab for viewing/editing daily routine
- Weekly review
- Prayer Journal, Verse Memorisation, Intercession Network
- Pillar Balance Radar chart
- Trends view with history
- Season system
- Pets & Mounts, Guild, Rewards Shop
- Accountability sharing
- Glass morphism UI with dark/light mode and time-of-day adaptive aurora
- Cloud sync via Firebase + local storage fallback

### Top Competitors and What They Do Well

Research the following apps and what makes them top-rated:

1. **Habitica** — Full RPG gamification, party quests, boss battles, social accountability, HP loss for missed dailies
2. **Streaks** — Minimalist elegance, beautiful streak visualisation, loss aversion as primary motivator
3. **Forest** — Virtual tree grows during focus sessions, real trees planted through partnership, novel metaphor
4. **Finch** — Positive reinforcement over punishment, virtual pet companion, mental health focus, avoids streak anxiety
5. **Fabulous** — Duke University behavioural science, coached journeys, habit stacking approach
6. **Productive** — Data-rich analytics, detailed weekly/monthly stats, excellent visualisations
7. **Strides** — Four distinct tracker types (Target, Habit, Average, Milestones), 150+ templates
8. **TickTick** — All-in-one (tasks + habits + Pomodoro + calendar), built-in focus timer with white noise
9. **Routinery** — Timer-based routine execution, step-by-step guided flows for morning/evening
10. **HabitBee** — AI mood tracking, correlates mood with habit completion, emotional intelligence
11. **Disciplined** — Google Play Best App 2024, ultra-clean UI, simplicity-first approach
12. **Loop Habit Tracker** — Completely free, calendar heatmaps, circle visualisations

### Common User Complaints About Discipline Apps
- Streak pressure causing burnout and all-or-nothing thinking
- No accommodation for real life (illness, travel, bad days)
- Notifications that are too easy to ignore
- Lack of personalised insights or pattern recognition
- One-size-fits-all approach
- Overly punitive mechanics that destroy motivation after one miss
- Apps that track but don't help you *understand* what's working

### What I Want From You

Based on this research, give me **specific, actionable suggestions** for improving PVA. For each suggestion:

1. **Feature name** — What to call it
2. **Inspiration** — Which app(s) inspired it and why it works
3. **How it fits PVA** — How it integrates with PVA's Christian faith framework and PVA model
4. **Implementation priority** — High / Medium / Low
5. **User impact** — What problem it solves or what behaviour it encourages

Focus on suggestions that:
- Strengthen the unique faith-based identity (no other top app has this)
- Improve daily engagement and reduce abandonment
- Add depth without overwhelming complexity
- Address common complaints from the discipline app space
- Make the gamification more meaningful (not just points for points' sake)
- Help users *understand* their patterns, not just track them

Give me 10-15 suggestions, ranked by impact. Be specific — don't just say "add social features," tell me exactly what kind and how it ties into PVA's framework.
